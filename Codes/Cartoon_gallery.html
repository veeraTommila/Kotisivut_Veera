Cartoon characters of mine

Here are some cartoon characters of mine. They are from my cartoon called “Nomi to Sini Noki no hanashi”. It means a story of Nomi Noki and Sini Noki. The Noki-family is suspected for a crime in a meat boutique in the Hitachu-city. The children of the family manage to escape from the jail far away to home of their mother's mother and father in Fuzu. On the trip to Fuzu Nomi and Sini will meet every kind of creatures and things.


After finally getting their mother’s parents they will familiarize themselves with creatures. At further parts of the cartoon tale the real robber of the meat boutique will be seized in Hitachu and the girls’ parents will get free for innocent. The girls’ mother (Aino Noki) and father (Hirohito Noki) will get to move to their children, but soon there will start war against the enemy government behind the great ocean-kind of lake as there are otherwise war going all around that world.

Here is Nomi Noki. She’s got ears of a cat and few whiskers on her cheeks. She’s also got dark green eyes; Sini’s got blue eyes. Their names are based of the colour of their eyes. Nomi’s name comes twisted from a Japanese word “Momi no ki (樅の木)”, which means spruce tree. Actually Nomi’s got spruce green eyes. Sini’s name come from a Finnish word “sini”, which means blue. She is Nomi’s little sister Sini Noki. There is a blue hue in this picture coming from Kaa. Kaa is a waterflame-fairy, who brings bluerly dim light to the room where Nomi and Sini sleeps. Here is Inga Žuuyu. She is a shurimanja -girl and the becoming protector/guardian of Nomi and Sini. Because Inga is a shurimanja, she has an odd face like that. As a shurimanja she spreads her skin of her face to a cloth and knots it on the top of her head. Her arms and legs are accordion-kind and flexible as springs. With those kind of legs she may jump on the top a high tree from the ground. She lives with her wide family on the neighbor of Nomi and Sini behind a small forest.
This is Kaa, a waterflame-fairy. She is the “heart” of the home of parents of mother of Sini and Noki. Together with a lysosome called Lucicha she takes care of for example the heating, tidiness and functional of the house of Noki’s and Sini’s grandparents. Although waterflames are water fairy, they can produce heat and light like the fireplaces. The brothers Aida and Tôutôu, the partners of the future of Nomi and Sini. Aida is the younger brother and Tôutôu is the elder one. They are wearing the autumn period school uniform of their school. Inga’s little brother Iiro Žuuyu is a protector of Aida and Tôutôu. A juudenkigen-dog called Lio. She’s got the red symbols of an imaginationed tosukawaisme-religion on her forehead. The word “juu” comes from Japanese by meaning of 10, "denki" = electricity and "gen" is an onyoomi (chinese founded) for the word "inu", which means dog. Juudenkigen means ten-tailed dog.
Lio’s got 10 tails. She lives on the grounds of Inga and she is a kind of a pet of Inga’s family. The handwritten numbers at the larger picture are the dates from 2008. It is the time when I have done the drawings.
A green lysosome called Lucicha. She lives in one hot water spring of the house of Nomi’s and Sini’s grandparents and takes care of cleaning the springs. In this case it’s necessary if one knows what do the lysozomes of animal cells do. Aino Noki, the hopeless mother of Sini and Nomi sitting on a chair in a jail.
